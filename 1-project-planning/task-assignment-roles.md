# Task Assignment & Roles
## Cybersecurity Incident Response Framework
**Document Type:** Task Assignment & Roles | **Section:** 1 — Project Planning & Management

---

## 1. Team Overview

This project is divided across 4 team members, each assigned a primary role with clear ownership of specific deliverables across all 4 weeks. Every member contributes to every week, but each person leads one domain.

---

## 2. Role Definitions and Responsibilities

### 🔵 Role 1 — Research Lead

**Core responsibility:** Finding, analyzing, and synthesizing all information the team builds on.

| Week | Tasks | Deliverables Owned |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| Week 1 | Research CIA Triad, threat types (phishing, ransomware, malware, DDoS, insider threats), and network discovery techniques. Collect minimum 5 reliable sources. | Research notes + source bibliography |
| Week 2 | Research NIST SP 800-61 and SANS PICERL frameworks. Study RBAC, MFA, least privilege. Summarize 6 IR phases. | IR framework research summary |
| Week 3 | Design the incident scenario: attack type, affected systems, attacker goals, expected outcomes. Share with Role 2 before simulation day. | Incident scenario document |
| Week 4 | Write Executive Summary (1 page max) and Introduction section of final report. Verify all citations are consistent throughout. | Executive summary + introduction |

**Skills required:** Research, reading academic sources, synthesis, citation management.

---

### 🟢 Role 2 — Technical Lead

**Core responsibility:** Owning all technical work — architecture, tools, and simulation execution.

| Week | Tasks | Deliverables Owned |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| Week 1 | Research and document network discovery techniques (Nmap, port scanning, enumeration) hands-on. Review Role 3's report for technical accuracy. | Network discovery technical section |
| Week 2 | Design secure network architecture (5-zone diagram). Write system hardening checklist (40+ controls). **Deliver architecture to Role 3 by Day 3–4 — this is a hard dependency.** | Architecture diagram + hardening report |
| Week 3 | Read Role 1's scenario. Execute the simulation step by step. Log every action with timestamps in real time. All 4 members must be present. | Live simulation execution log |
| Week 4 | Write all technical sections of final report: architecture decisions, hardening results, simulation findings, technical lessons learned. | Technical sections of final report |

**Skills required:** Network architecture, security tools, hands-on technical work, documentation under pressure.

---

### 🟡 Role 3 — Documentation Lead

**Core responsibility:** Turning everyone's work into polished, structured, formally written documents.

| Week | Tasks | Deliverables Owned |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| Week 1 | Wait for Role 1 + Role 2 inputs (by Day 5). Write and format the full Week 1 report with academic structure. Send draft to all members before submission. | Full Week 1 report document |
| Week 2 | Write the formal IR Plan document using Role 1's framework research and Role 2's architecture diagram. Cannot start until Role 2 delivers diagram (Day 3–4). | Formal IR Plan document |
| Week 3 | Be present on simulation day. Take live notes. After simulation, write the full incident simulation report including containment, eradication, and recovery sections. | Simulation report + response documentation |
| Week 4 | Collect sections from Role 1 and Role 2. Merge all into one final cohesive report. Ensure consistent formatting, font, headings, and page numbers throughout. | Compiled final report |

**Skills required:** Technical writing, document structure, formatting, academic standards, quality assurance.

---

### 🟣 Role 4 — Presenter Lead

**Core responsibility:** Converting all documents into professional slides and leading the presentation.

| Week | Tasks | Deliverables Owned |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| Week 1 | After Role 3 finishes the Week 1 report, convert it into 5 presentation slides with speaker notes. Present to team for feedback. | Week 1 presentation slides + speaker notes |
| Week 2 | Help Role 3 with document formatting. Draft a visual IR plan phase diagram slide. Build Week 2 section of master deck. | IR plan visual slide |
| Week 3 | Be present on simulation day. Observe and capture key moments. After simulation, build Week 3 results slides including visual attack timeline. | Simulation results slides |
| Week 4 | Build the complete 14-slide final presentation deck. Write detailed speaker notes for every slide. Lead or co-lead the live presentation. All members do mock run-through by Day 5. | Final presentation deck + all speaker notes |

**Skills required:** Visual design, PowerPoint/slide creation, public speaking, summarizing complex content visually.

---

## 3. Collaboration Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Role 2 hard deadline | Architecture diagram delivered to Role 3 by **Week 2, Day 3–4** — no exceptions. Delays block the IR Plan. |
| Simulation day | **ALL 4 members must be present** for the Week 3 simulation. Not optional. |
| No solo submissions | No deliverable is submitted without the full team reviewing it first. |
| Dependency chain | Role 1 + Role 2 → Role 3 → Role 4. Each week follows this flow. |
| Week 2 gates Week 3 | Week 3 simulation cannot run until Week 2 IR Plan is fully complete. |
